The University of Washington sailing team is a student-lead competitive sailing team. We primarily race in cFJs, but we also sail compete in lasers and J22s. We are a club sport representing the University of Washington in the Inter-collegiate Sailing Association. Our women’s and co-ed teams are top-ranked in our division—the Northwest Inter-collegiate Sailing Association (NWISCA). The team practices at Sail Sand Point on Tuesdays, Wednesdays, and Fridays. We have around 20 active members, but we’re always looking for more.

The Husky Sailing team is a student-lead organization. This means the students on the team do all the team’s fundraising, scheduling, practice, organization, etcetera. The team is led by two co-captains, currently Connor Hughes and Audrey Jacobs. The team treasurer—Nathaniel Gordon—is responsible for the team’s financial management. The fundraiser manger, currently Shaan Shridhar, organizes team fundraisers, most notably the team auction. Hayden Potter is responsible for coordinating practices as the team Practice Coordinator. Erik Skeel is the person in charge of planning and purchasing team gear. Farzana Mohamedali is the current Recruitment Officer. Cora Knauss is the person behind the team’s social media account. The website is maintained and build by Austin Hauter. And last but not least, housing for the team, and housing for other teams when they travel to Seattle is managed by Jo Garcia.

The UW Sailing Team is one of ten teams in NWICSA. In the Fall and Spring the team participates in regattas against the other teams in our division. The team also sends sailors to compete in inter-conference regattas and championships. During spring quarter, the team goes to a regatta every weekend.
